Facing Lebanon's economic crisis and high female unemployment, Puck launched "Selfless Shelves" to empower five women entrepreneurs. Puck repurposed its cheese jars, providing them to women from different villages to package their homemade traditional foods ('mouneh'). These specially packaged jars were then given prime space on Puck's own shelves in Carrefour supermarkets across Lebanon, offering these women a vital platform and income source. The initiative turned everyday jars into symbols of resilience and economic independence, moving beyond just selling products to actively supporting communities and highlighting inspiring personal stories. It's a bold move demonstrating how a brand can create significant local social and economic impact through creative thinking and reallocating resources for good. 🛍️🤝


🎨 Creative Agencies: The campaign was led by FP7 McCann and Current Global MENAT, with production by Craft UAE and Très Content.

🍶 Packaging/Visuals: Focus on the reuse of the iconic Puck jars and likely the design of labels or tags that told the women's individual stories. The campaign heavily featured visual storytelling, including webisodes about the women.

🏆 Recognition: It's won major design and creative awards like Cannes Lions (Bronze in Creative Commerce, Titanium shortlist) and D&AD (Bronze in Commerce). This highlights the strong creative execution.

💡 Purpose-Driven Design: The core design challenge was transforming a standard product container into a vehicle for social empowerment and storytelling, making the packaging itself a key part of the message.
